01 — Abstract

The short version

I'm a Pharm.D.-trained clinical researcher who spent nine years moving between the ward and the writing desk — dispensing medications one year, drafting WHO guideline chapters the next. My throughline is evidence synthesis: taking scattered trial data, stewardship logs, and clinical questions, and turning them into recommendations that clinicians can actually act on.

I'm currently a Project Research Scientist III at PGIMER Chandigarh, reviewing evidence for national antibiotic guidelines and training the next generation of guideline developers. Before that, I built and ran an antifungal stewardship program at Christian Medical College, Vellore, and co-authored WHO's global recommendations on echinococcosis and meningitis care.

Off the clock: I'm the person who read the fine print on 3 systematic review software licenses so my team didn't have to, and once got a "Best Oral Presentation" award for explaining a meta-analysis with enough enthusiasm to win over a room of skeptical epidemiologists.
